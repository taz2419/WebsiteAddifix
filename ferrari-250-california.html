Ferrari 250 GT California Spyder

Premium vintage car parts via additive manufacturing

The Ferrari 250 GT California Spyder is an iconic open-top sports car that represents the golden age of automotive engineering. Produced from 1958 to 1962, this legendary vehicle was designed by Luigi Chinetti as a response to John Von Neumann's proposal for a Spider adapted to California's sunny climate.

Built primarily with sheet steel and aluminum doors, with select models featuring all-aluminum construction by Scaglietti, the 250 California was produced in two distinct series: the "Long Wheelbase" (1958-1960) and the "Short Wheelbase" (1960-1962). Its timeless design and exceptional performance make it one of the most sought-after classic Ferraris.
